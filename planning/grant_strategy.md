# Grant Justification and Application Strategy  
*Project: From Biological Naturalism to Epistemic Limits: Reinterpreting the Chinese Room Argument*

---

## 1. Purpose of This Document

These notes outline the rationale, targets, and strategy for pursuing grant funding to support the writing and publication of my paper on reinterpreting John Searle’s **Chinese Room Argument** as an **epistemological** rather than **ontological** claim.  
The goal is to secure short-term research support (3–6 months) for focused philosophical work connecting **philosophy of mind**, **AI ethics**, and **epistemology of consciousness**.

---

## 2. Project Summary (for Reference)

**Title:** *From Biological Naturalism to Epistemic Limits: Reinterpreting the Chinese Room Argument*  
**Thesis:** Searle’s argument has been misread as claiming that computers cannot think; properly understood, it identifies epistemic limits on how we can *know* or *test* for consciousness.  
**Core Claim:**  
1. We don’t know what consciousness is.  
2. We don’t know how to test for it.  
3. We can’t determine consciousness from behavior alone.  

**Deliverables:**
- 10–12k word research paper (target: *Minds and Machines* or *Synthese*).  
- Public summary article for cross-disciplinary readership (AI ethics / philosophy).  
- Annotated database of Searle’s epistemic statements (1980–2015).  

**Timeline:** ~6 months total (textual analysis → drafting → peer review).

## 3. Why This Project Deserves Funding

### Intellectual Merit
- Provides a **new interpretive framework** for one of philosophy’s most cited thought experiments.  
- Resolves contradictions in Searle’s biological naturalism by showing its **epistemic continuity**.  
- Bridges **20th-century philosophy of mind** and **21st-century AI consciousness debates**.

### Broader Impact
- Clarifies foundational assumptions used in **AI consciousness policy** and **ethics discussions**.  
- Contributes to ongoing public and academic reassessment of Searle and Dennett (both deceased 2024).  
- Demonstrates how classic philosophical arguments can guide **evaluation frameworks for LLMs** and emerging AI systems.  

### Funding Justification
- This is a **low-cost, high-impact humanities project**.  
- Main needs: time for research, conference travel, and publication support.  
- Aligns well with funders interested in **AI, mind, and epistemology**.

## 5. Framing Strategy

### A. Core Framing Idea
> “Reinterpreting the Chinese Room Argument as an epistemic challenge to AI consciousness testing — bridging philosophy of mind, epistemology, and AI ethics.”

### B. Emphasize Interdisciplinary Relevance
- Connect classical philosophy (Searle, Dennett, Chalmers) with **current AI consciousness research**.  
- Frame the project as clarifying **conceptual foundations** for responsible AI evaluation.  
- Highlight **policy relevance** (behavioral testing, consciousness attribution, precautionary ethics).

### C. Avoid Common Pitfalls
- Don’t frame this as a purely historical study — emphasize contemporary implications.  
- Position as **clarificatory scholarship**, not biographical or commemorative.  
- Show **practical stakes**: understanding consciousness affects how we treat AI systems.
