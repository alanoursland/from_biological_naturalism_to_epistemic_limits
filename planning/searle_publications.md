## **Section 3: Selected Interviews and Lectures**

Searle often clarified his positions in public lectures and interviews, which provide accessible entry points into his thought.

* **Interview with Julian Moore** (1999), *Philosophy Now*, Issue 25.
  Discusses coherence across his theories of speech acts, mind, and social reality, with an informal restatement of the Chinese Room argument. [11]

* **TEDxCERN Talk: “Consciousness and the Brain”** (2013).
  Presents his theory of biological naturalism and introduces the *observer-relative* vs. *observer-independent* distinction as a critique of computational theories of mind. [12]

* **Conversation with John Searle** (2015), *The John W. Kluge Center*, Library of Congress.
  Reflects on the evolution of philosophy and his core theoretical motivations. [13]
