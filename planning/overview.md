# Paper Plan: From Biological Naturalism to Epistemic Limits: Reinterpreting the Chinese Room Argument

## Executive Summary

This paper reinterprets John Searle's Chinese Room argument as primarily an epistemological claim about the limits of behavioral tests for consciousness, rather than an ontological claim about what can or cannot be conscious. This interpretation resolves apparent contradictions in Searle's work and has significant implications for current debates about AI consciousness.

## Core Thesis

The Chinese Room argument is commonly misinterpreted as claiming that computational systems cannot have consciousness or understanding. This paper argues that Searle's actual position, particularly in his later work, is the more modest epistemological claim that behavioral tests cannot definitively establish the presence of consciousness or understanding.
